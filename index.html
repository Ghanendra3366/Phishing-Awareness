Phishing Awareness

Learn how to spot phishing emails and protect yourself online.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of online scam where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations via email, text message, or other means to steal sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details.

How to Detect Phishing Emails

  • Check the sender's email address for misspellings or suspicious domains.
  • Look for urgent or threatening language that pressures you to act quickly.
  • Hover over links to see where they really lead before clicking.
  • Beware of unexpected attachments or requests for personal information.
  • Check for poor grammar and spelling mistakes.

Real Email Examples

Phishing Example

From: support@micros0ft-secure.com

Subject: Urgent: Your Account Will Be Locked!

Dear user,
We noticed suspicious activity in your account. Please click here to verify your information or your account will be suspended.
Thank you,
Microsoft Support Team

  • Notice the misspelled sender domain (micros0ft-secure.com instead of microsoft.com).
  • Urgent language and threats.
  • Suspicious link.

Legitimate Example

From: support@microsoft.com

Subject: Security Alert: Unusual Sign-in Activity

Hello,
We detected a sign-in attempt from a new device. If this was you, no action is needed. If not, please visit your account at https://account.microsoft.com.
Thank you,
Microsoft Account Team

  • Official sender address.
  • No threats or urgent demands.
  • Directs you to log in via the official website.
